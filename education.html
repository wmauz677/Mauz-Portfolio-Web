Education

Colorado School of Mines

I am currently a Senior at Mines studying Mechanical Engineering and I expect to graduate in May, 2019. I came to this school because I love Colorado and because Mines is a highly regarded engineering school. Some of the most valuable classes that I have taken include:

MEGN 498 - Vehicle Dynamics : This course encompasses automotive engineering, and is taught as a "beginner" class into the performance of cars. The course teaches the different components of vehicles, and shows how to alter their configuration and manufacturing to produce results for differnet applications.

CSCI 261 / 262 - Programming Concepts / Data Structures: These two computer science courses are taught in C++. They started with the basics of the language, eventually teaching code performance optimization to produce the most computationally efficient programs.

PHGN 100/200 - Physics I and II encompassed all of the basic engineering principals that we use in all of our other classes. The understanding of all future engineering classes rely on the base knowledge learned in mechanics and electromagnestism from these two classes

MATH 111/112/213/225 - Calculus I,II, III, and Differential Equations are especially important for any engineer. Just as physics I and II, they provide the base of knowledge necessary to understand future engineering courses
